  - question: 'What makes scaffolding "complex" compared to standard scaffolding?'
    answer: 'Complex scaffolding involves non-standard configurations such as cantilevered sections, suspended platforms, independent free-standing structures, or work on heritage buildings, irregular architectures, bridges, and environmentally sensitive sites. These projects require specialised engineering design, innovative problem-solving, and enhanced compliance documentation.'
  - question: 'Do you have experience with heritage building scaffolding in Christchurch?'
    answer: 'Yes. We have extensive experience providing scaffolding for Christchurch heritage buildings including churches, historic commercial facades, and character homes. We follow Heritage New Zealand guidance, implement non-invasive support systems, and ensure reversible installation methods that protect sensitive historic fabric.'
  - question: 'What is cantilever scaffolding and when is it needed?'
    answer: 'Cantilever scaffolding projects from a building face without ground support beneath. It is used when ground access is restricted, such as over public footpaths, above sensitive landscaping, or spanning archaeological sites. Cantilever systems use counterweight or building-anchored needle beams and always require engineering certification.'
  - question: 'How do you protect archaeological or heritage sites during scaffolding installation?'
    answer: 'We use independent free-standing structures that avoid contact with sensitive ground, implement surface protection measures, distribute loads to prevent point-loading, follow conservation plan requirements, and maintain full documentation. We coordinate with Heritage New Zealand and archaeologists as required.'
  - question: 'How long does the design process take for complex scaffolding projects?'
    answer: 'Complex scaffolding typically requires 1-2 weeks of design development including site assessment, engineering calculations, stakeholder consultation, and certification. Heritage projects may take longer due to conservation plan integration and heritage authority approvals. We begin the process as early as possible to avoid project delays.'
