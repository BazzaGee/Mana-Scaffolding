## Understanding Scaffolding Pricing

Scaffolding costs vary significantly based on project requirements. This guide helps you understand what drives pricing and how to budget effectively for your scaffolding needs.

### Base Pricing Structure

**Residential Scaffolding:**
- Standard house (single storey exterior): $40-80 per linear metre per week
- Two-storey house: $60-120 per linear metre per week
- Roof access platforms: $300-800 per week
- Minimum hire periods typically 1-2 weeks

**Commercial Scaffolding:**
- Office buildings: $80-150 per square metre per month
- Complex structures: $150-300 per square metre per month
- Tower scaffold systems: $200-500 per day

**Industrial Scaffolding:**
- Plant shutdown work: Quoted per project based on complexity
- Specialized access: Custom pricing required
- Long-term installations: Negotiated rates available

### Factors Affecting Cost

**1. Height and Complexity**
- Every additional level increases cost by approximately 15-25%
- Complex shapes and confined spaces require more labour
- Cantilever and suspended systems cost significantly more

**2. Duration**
- Longer hire periods often reduce weekly rates
- Short-term hires (under 1 week) may incur minimum charges
- Seasonal demand can affect availability and pricing

**3. Access and Site Conditions**
- Difficult access increases installation time and cost
- Sloping ground requires additional foundations
- Public footpath protection adds permit and equipment costs
- Traffic management may be required

**4. Load Requirements**
- Light duty (225 kg/m²): Standard residential/commercial
- Medium duty (450 kg/m²): Heavy materials storage
- Heavy duty (675 kg/m²): Industrial applications
- Special loads: Custom engineering required

**5. Location**
- Urban areas: Higher costs due to traffic management
- Remote locations: Travel and accommodation costs
- Canterbury region: Generally competitive market rates

| Item | Typical Cost |

| After-hours work | +25-50% |
| Weekend work | +50-100% |

### Getting Accurate Quotes

**Information to Provide:**
1. Site address and photos
2. Building dimensions (height, length, width)
3. Purpose of scaffolding
4. Expected duration
5. Access constraints
6. Any special requirements

**Questions to Ask Providers:**
- What is included in the quote?
- Are there additional fees for alterations?
- What notice is required for changes?
- Is dismantling included?
- What insurance coverage is provided?

### Hidden Costs to Avoid

**Unexpected expenses often include:**
- Extra weeks when projects run over
- Damage repairs (ensure insurance covers this)
- Permit delays extending hire periods
- Rush fees for urgent installations
- Non-standard equipment requests

### Value vs. Price

The cheapest quote isn't always the best value. Consider:
- **Experience:** Do they understand your project type?
- **Safety record:** What is their incident history?
- **Insurance:** Is coverage adequate for your risk?
- **Reliability:** Can they commit to your timeline?
- **References:** What do past clients say?
